Interested in becoming a student leader at the University of Mary Washington? Apply to the<strong>Â Presidential Emerging Leaders Program </strong>and you may be eligible to receive a $15,000 scholarship your first year at UMW.
<h3>Purpose</h3>
<ul>
 	<li>To provide a formal leadership development opportunity for first-year students that will enhance their first-year experience and prepare students to be stronger leaders on campus</li>
 	<li>To present students with various leadership opportunities available to them on campus</li>
 	<li>To connect students to a variety of influencers at the University, including President Paino</li>
</ul>
<h3>Learning Outcomes</h3>
<ul>
 	<li>Participants will discover the many opportunities to get involved in and around campus</li>
 	<li>Participants will explore what kind of leader they aspire to be by exploring different leadership opportunities available to them.</li>
 	<li>Participants will reflect and learn more about their personal identities and their role in the community.</li>
 	<li>Participants will identify key elements of appropriate and healthy interpersonal relationships.</li>
</ul>
<h3>Selection Process</h3>
<ul>
 	<li>Application
<ul>
 	<li>Essay questions
<ul>
 	<li>Show/tell us who you are</li>
 	<li>Who do you admire in your life and why?</li>
</ul>
</li>
 	<li>Letter of Recommendation (optional)</li>
</ul>
</li>
 	<li>Students who apply Early Action and Early Decision are given priority acceptance to the program but is still open to students who apply regular decision</li>
</ul>
<h3>Program Details</h3>
<ul>
 	<li>Students are not required to live in the same residence hall or participate in the same First Year Seminar course (FSEM).</li>
 	<li>Participants will move on campus the day before other first-year students arrive to campus. Students will get an introduction to leadership and spend time with upper-level administrators. They will also spend time with out students in the program between New Student Arrival activities.</li>
 	<li>The program will run through April of their first year.</li>
 	<li>Curriculum will include bi-weekly or monthly sessions on a variety of leadership topics presented by staff and faculty, discussions, and social events.</li>
 	<li>Participants will be encouraged to attend programs and events such as Student Leadership Summit, the Social Justice and Leadership Summit, COAR service events, etc.</li>
 	<li>Participants will receive a certificate of completion at the end of the year after attending the sessions, discussions, and events.</li>
</ul>
